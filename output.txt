Vocab 41-50 – Alex Wohlbruck – Feb 23, 2018

1. Banal (Adjective)
so lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring
trite, hackneyed, clichéd
"As long as domesticated TV reigns supreme, our fascination with the banal will surely continue."

2. Hackneyed (Adjective)
(of a phrase or idea) having been overused; unoriginal and trite
overused, overworked, overdone
"His boss can take even a hackneyed phrase and let it dangle suggestively in the air until a dozen meanings reveal themselves."

3. Trite (Adjective)
(of a remark or idea) lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse
hackneyed, banal, clichéd
"The lyrics are trite and valuable ink has been wasted in printing them."

4. Overture (Noun)
an orchestral piece at the beginning of an opera, play, etc.
prelude, introduction, opening
"Recent overtures by extremist groups to renounce violence and discuss issues provides a window of opportunity to redress old wrongs."

5. Prelude (Noun)
an action or event serving as an introduction to something more important
preliminary, overture, opening
"The channel will telecast exclusive footage on Nikita on June 1 at 9 p.m. as a prelude to the telecast of the event."

6. Prologue (Noun)
a separate introductory section of a literary, dramatic, or musical work
introduction, foreword, preface
"It will be the first time trial of next year's Tour, apart from the short prologue, and race organizers hope the idea will keep the race alive until the latest possible date."

7. Preamble (Noun)
a preliminary or preparatory statement; an introduction
introduction, opening remarks, preliminary remarks
"It is clear that the provisions of the preamble and of Article 1 of the charter which are claimed to be in conflict with the alien land law are not self-executing."

8. Preface (Noun)
an introduction to a book, typically stating its subject, scope, or aims.
introduction, foreword, preamble
"I preface my remarks by saying that I do not like the fact that our tuition is going up."

9. Coda (Noun)
the concluding passage of a piece or movement, typically forming an addition to the basic structure
"The sad coda to this event is that, a mere five months later, this disaster is already a fading memory."

10. Epilogue (Noun)
a section or speech at the end of a book or play that serves as a comment on or a conclusion to what has happened
afterword, postscript, PS
"He leaves the house with the curse of his father, but in the epilogue to the play, his family accepts his decision."
