1. Covert (Adjective)
not openly acknowledged or displayed
secret, furtive, clandestine
"During this period a few Swedish divers had carried out covert night dives on the wrecks."

2. Stealthy (Adjective)
behaving or done in a cautious and surreptitious manner, so as not to be seen or heard
furtive, secretive, secret
"Ironically, stealthy design also makes for a less efficient weapon of war."

3. Surreptitious (Adjective)
kept secret, especially because it would not be approved of
secret, stealthy, clandestine
"There appears to be an increasing amount of surveillance, much of it surreptitious."

4. Clandestine (Adjective)
kept secret or done secretively, especially because illicit
secret, covert, furtive
"She was a clandestine officer of the CIA and she had worked undercover around the world."

5. Allay (Verb)
diminish or put at rest (fear, suspicion, or worry)
reduce, diminish, decrease
"And any idea or thought that allays this fear is likely to have a huge influence on human conduct."

6. Alleviate (Verb)
make (suffering, deficiency, or a problem) less severe
reduce, ease, relieve
"It alleviated the problem a little, but not enough to stave off looming financial disaster."

7. Assuage (Verb)
make (an unpleasant feeling) less intense
relieve, ease, alleviate
"Trying to assuage the ruffled feelings of the masses by conducting such events in situations of necessity may be fine."

8. Appease (Verb)
pacify or placate (someone) by acceding to their demands
conciliate, placate, pacify
"Apparently dancing with a gay guy appeases a girl's desire to dance, which at the same time is not construed as flirtation."

9. Mitigate (Verb)
make (something bad) less severe, serious, or painful
alleviate, reduce, diminish
"So government will have to take some sort of measures to mitigate this."

10. Mollify (Verb)
appease the anger or anxiety of (someone)
appease, placate, pacify
"It was a so-so show - certainly not enough to mollify the effects of standing in a freezing queue for 40 minutes."
